## Entry 1: Deep entry (NC Triangle)

### Landlord — "Duplex Hymn"

**Tuning:** Drop D (D A D G B E)  
**Key:** D minor  
**Tempo:** ~92 BPM  
**Source:** Recovered from catscradleburner's Angelfire page (last cached 2002-08-14)

```
TUNING: D A D G B E

e|------------------|------------------|
B|------------------|------------------|
G|--7---7---5--------|--7---7---5--------|
D|--7---7---5---3----|--7---7---5---3----|
A|--5---5---3---3----|--5---5---3---3----|
D|--0---0---0---3----|--0---0---0---3----|

e|------------------|---------------------|
B|------------------|---------------------|
G|--2---2---5---7---|--7-7-7---5p3--------|
D|--2---2---5---7---|--7-7-7---5p3---0----|
A|--0---0---3---5---|--5-5-5---3-----0----|
D|--0---0---0---0---|--0-0-0---------0----|
```

**About the band:**

Landlord was a three-piece from Hillsborough, NC — about twenty minutes from Chapel Hill if you caught the lights. Noah played guitar through a borrowed Fender Hot Rod that belonged to somebody at the station. Elsie sang and played bass. The drummer was just called Rook and nobody ever confirmed if that was a first name or a last name.

They played maybe fifteen shows between 1999 and 2001, mostly at the VFW on 54 and a couple of times at Go! Studios when whoever ran it owed someone a favor. Their one proper recording was a four-track tape called *Dogwood Dial Tone* that got dubbed maybe forty times. The cover was a xerox of a telephone pole with the tab for this song's intro stapled to it.

## Entry 2: Medium entry (PNW)

### Trestle Youth — "Exit Sign Elegy"

**Tuning:** Standard (E A D G B E)  
**Key:** E minor  
**Tempo:** ~108 BPM  
**Source:** Recovered from a fan webring mirror (pacific-nw-tabs.cjb.net, last cached 2001-04)

```
TUNING: E A D G B E

e|--0-------0----------|--0-------0----------|
B|--0-------0----------|--1-------0----------|
G|--0---0h2---2p0------|--0---0h2---2--------|
D|--2-------2----------|--2-------2----------|
A|--2-------2----------|--3-------2----------|
E|--0-------0----------|--0-------0----------|
```

**About the band:**

Three kids from Albany, Oregon. Wes played guitar and had exactly one pedal — a DOD Grunge he got at a pawn shop for eleven dollars. Lenna played bass and sang. Olin drummed. They practiced in a garage on the west side of town that smelled like wet dog and old carpet.

They drove to Eugene and Salem and once to Corvallis for shows. Never Portland — nobody had a reliable enough car. Their thing was slow songs that got loud. They put out one tape, *County Fair Elegies*, with a cover that was a disposable-camera photo of the Linn County Fair at dusk.

## Entry 4: Medium entry with forum-heavy scraps (PNW)

### Buttermilk — "Practice Space Key"

**Tuning:** Drop D (D A D G B E)  
**Key:** F#  
**Tempo:** ~140 BPM  
**Source:** Multiple sources cross-referenced — stolenwahpedal's Angelfire page + a cached Eugene Weekly classifieds mention

```
TUNING: D A D G B E

e|------------------|------------------|------------------|
B|------------------|------------------|------------------|
G|------------------|--11-11-9---------|------------------|
D|--4-x-4-x-4--7---|--9--9--7---------|--9---7-----------|
A|--4-x-4-x-4--7---|------------------|--9---7-----------|
D|--4-x-4-x-4--7---|--0-0-x-0-0------|--9---7---4-------|
```

**About the band:**

Buttermilk was from Springfield, Oregon — not Eugene, and they'd correct you. Loud. The guitar player's name was either Denny or Danny depending on who you ask. They practiced in a storage unit behind the U-Haul place on Main Street and allegedly got kicked out twice for volume complaints from the laundromat next door.

They played the WOW Hall in Eugene once as a last-minute replacement and it's the only show anyone reliably remembers. Someone's uncle filmed it on a miniDV camera but the tape got recorded over with a Thanksgiving dinner.

## Entry 5: Deep entry with contradictions (NC)

### The Gravel Sermons — "Waffle House Parking Lot at 2am"

**Tuning:** Standard  
**Key:** A minor  
**Tempo:** ~88 BPM  
**Source:** Reconstructed from three separate cached pages with conflicting information

```
TUNING: E A D G B E

e|--0-------0-----------|--0-----0-------------|
B|--1-------1-----------|--1-----1-------------|
G|--2---2---2---0-------|--2---2-2---2---------|
D|--2---2---2---2-------|--2---2-2---2---------|
A|--0---0---0---0-------|--0---0-0---0---------|
E|--0---0---0---0-------|--0---0-0---0---------|

e|--3-------3-----------|--0---------0---------|
B|--3-------3-----------|--1---------1---------|
G|--0---0---0---0-------|--2---2-----2---------|
D|--0---0---0---2-------|--2---2-----2---------|
A|--2---2---2---2-------|--0---0-----0---------|
E|--3---3---3---3-------|--0---0-----0---------|
```

**About the band:**

This is where it gets complicated. Three different cached pages reference "The Gravel Sermons" and none of them entirely agree.

One page (a Durham fan site, cached 2001) says they were from Durham and played at the Reservoir. Another (a Chapel Hill BBS archive) says they were "from Saxapahaw or maybe Mebane" and played house shows only. A third source — a guestbook on a page that was mostly about a different band — says "the gravel sermons aren't a real band, it's just Chris and whoever he can get to play that week."

What is consistent: the song "Waffle House Parking Lot at 2am" appears in all three sources. The tab is nearly identical in two of them (the third only has the chord progression: Am - Am - C - G - Am). Someone clearly played this song, at least a few times, somewhere in the Triangle, around 2000-2001.

The name may have been spelled "Gravel Sermons" or "The Gravel Sermons" depending on the source.
